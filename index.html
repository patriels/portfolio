About Me

Research meets craft,
curiosity drives both.

I'm a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Florida. My research centers around technology designed and built for people, not the other way around. My work spans a variety of backgrounds including software engineering, human-computer interaction, applied artifical intelligence and simulation.

I strive to always center the user. This ensures that the work I do isn't just motivated by innovation, but that is serves to answer the how and why of building effective technoloigies. I bring this user-centered focus to all my projects. Whether that's building simulaions or designing user interfaces I ensure that my work always serves the human good.

My current work focuses on leveraging smart home technologies embued with agentic models to support geriactric person aging in place. I'm actively seeking collaborative opportunities where I can contribute to meaningful, real-world impact.
